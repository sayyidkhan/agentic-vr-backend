# NEXT Hackathon

36 hours. Over $200K in prizes, credits, and resources. Top 5 demo live on the SuperAI WEKA Stage.

NEXT Hackathon is SuperAI's flagship hackathon — 200 builders, equipped with the tools, mentors, and network to go from idea to working demo in a single sprint. The top 5 teams demo live on the WEKA stage at SuperAI, in front of 10,000 attendees.

## The Theme

Build anything — but make it agentic.

We don't care what problem you solve. We care how your agents solve it. This hackathon is about pushing the frontier of autonomous systems. Your project can tackle any domain — productivity, finance, healthcare, creative tools, infrastructure, whatever — as long as agents are doing real work at the core of it.

## The Stack

A production-ready agent needs infrastructure to run on, a frontend users can reach, access to live information, and the ability to take real-world actions. We've partnered with four tools that cover each of these layers — with free credits, hands-on workshops, and mentors on the floor throughout the hackathon.

AWS — Cloud infrastructure for your backend. Run compute, storage, databases, and managed AI services. Every team gets a temporary AWS account with full service access, plus 500 Kiro credits.

Vercel — Frontend deployment and AI tooling. Deploy your UI, use v0 to generate interfaces, and route LLM calls through the Vercel AI Gateway. $50 in v0 credits per team.

Exa — Web search and retrieval designed for agents. Returns clean, structured content from the live web. $100 in credits per team.

Stripe — Payments and financial workflows. Charge users, run subscriptions, or trigger payouts directly from your agent. $250 in credits per team.

## Judging Criteria

Submissions are evaluated across four dimensions:

- Agent Overview — What agent(s) did you build? What is their purpose?
- Autonomy & Decision-Making — How does your agent decide what to do next? What reasoning, planning, or tool-use patterns does it employ?
- Actions & Tool Use — What actions can your agent take? What tools/APIs/environments does it interact with?
- Orchestration — If multi-agent: how do your agents coordinate, delegate, or communicate?
- Human-in-the-Loop (if any) — Where does a human intervene, approve, or override?
- Failure Handling — How does your agent recover from errors or unexpected states?
- Demo & Presentation — Clarity of the pitch, quality of the demo/product, and how well the team communicates what they built.

## Submission Guidelines

Deadline: 12:00am on 11 June (i.e. Submit by 11:59pm on 10 June). No late entries allowed.

What to submit — every team includes:

- GitHub repo — public, or with judge access granted
- Project link — live URL or hosted demo
- Presentation slides — Google Drive link to a .ppt or .keynote file (see notes below)

A note on presentation slides — used if you're selected as a Top 5 finalist and pitch on the WEKA stage:

- Format must be .ppt or .keynote. Google Slides / Gamma / Vercel page links are not accepted — they won't play reliably on stage.
- If you want to showcase your demo in action, use a screen recording. Live demos are prohibited to avoid technical errors on stage. If you demo includes screen recordings, embed the video directly in your slides. Do not link to YouTube or external video.
- Slides are a visual aid for your live pitch. Keep them visual and punchy.
- Slides are locked at submission. No changes after the deadline, so make sure your deck is stage-ready before you submit.
